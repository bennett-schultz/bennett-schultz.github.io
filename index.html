About

Raised in Plainfield, Illinois, finance became a genuine interest early on, which led to building a personal investment portfolio as soon as it was possible. Markets are followed every morning, and free time is often spent on investment research for personal positions, including building concise, professional pitch decks to pressure-test convictions and communicate theses clearly.

Outside investing, interests include dirt biking, baseball, and cars, especially performance marques such as Ferrari and Aston Martin.

Investment Philosophy

Equities • Growth • Turnarounds • Catalysts

Equities are viewed as the strongest long-term asset class, and at this stage, the portfolio is intentionally concentrated toward them. Research is centered on two primary categories: high-quality growth names and turnaround situations where catalysts are clear, understandable, and explainable to others. A recurring focus is on businesses where recent pessimism appears overblown and the stock has repriced downward beyond fundamental impairment. The work also looks for setups where investor sentiment can shift meaningfully due to policy, contracts, or macro positioning, particularly within space and aerospace-adjacent names.

Examples of researched and invested names (entry prices shown): RKLB ($5.61), AMD ($78.69), ACHR ($3.70), CELH ($23.23), GOOGL ($154.30), ELF ($51.11), META ($483.51), LUNR ($5.51), ASTS ($18.84).
